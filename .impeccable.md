# Design Context — Jolene Mallen Designs

## Product
Portfolio site for Jolene Mallen, an interior designer in North Carolina (BFA Interior
Architecture, LEED Green Associate, pursuing NCIDQ). Rebuild of her existing Wix site
(jolenemallendesigns.com) — the work itself (renderings, sketches, textiles) is the star.

## Target audience
Hiring managers at design firms, potential clients, and industry peers. They arrive to
evaluate her work and credentials quickly — often from a phone, often from a LinkedIn link.

## Use cases
1. Browse project imagery (portfolio renderings, conceptual projects, sketches, textiles)
2. Read her background / credentials
3. View or download her resume
4. Reach her (email, LinkedIn)

## Brand personality
Quiet gallery confidence. The current site's voice: warm, unpretentious, craft-focused
(she crochets, throws pottery, cultivates houseplants) but professionally rigorous
(LEED, ADA, WELL certifications). Imagery is warm interiors, botanical greens, clay and
textile tones. Nothing loud, nothing techy, nothing corporate.

## Aesthetic direction
Two switchable themes chosen by Jolene in the CMS:
- **classic** — faithful to the current site: continuous scroll, numbered sections
  ("01 About"), tracked-uppercase geometric sans headings, paper-white space, images
  do the talking. Refined minimalism executed with precision.
- **studio** — editorial/architectural drafting-room take on the same content: oversized
  ghost numerals, asymmetric grids, hairline rules like drawing guides, serif display
  moments. Bolder, but still in service of the imagery.

Visitor-facing light/dark toggle; Jolene picks one of 4 palettes (gallery, sage,
terracotta, slate) in the CMS. Warm-tinted neutrals — never pure black/white, never
neon, no glassmorphism. Fonts self-hosted: Jost (geometric sans), Fraunces (studio serif).

## Constraints
Astro static output; images through astro:assets; content edited via Sveltia CMS
(git-based); must be excellent on mobile; no external CDNs (self-hosted fonts/scripts);
accessibility matters (she designs ADA-compliant spaces — her site should comply too).
